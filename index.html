Brooklyn Latin

High School• September 2015-June 2019

The Brooklyn Latin School

A specialized high school in which I pursue my International Baccalaureate (IB) diploma. My classes regularly hold socratic seminars in which each student leads question-and-answer discussions, and take part in declamation (public speaking) exercises. My experience so far has helped me gain confidence and think critically while pushing me to work hard in this academically rigorous environment. I am currently an IB diploma candidate taking Higher Level Math, Latin, and Arts courses along with other classes in the curriculum.

Work

Formation of Engineers: Fellowship

Fellow• September 2018- Present

The Formation of Engineers is a new initiative at The New York Hall of Science to research ways to improve engineering activities in the museum. My responsibilities include designing engineering-based activities for staff and visitors, iterating activities in Design Lab, and planning STEM events with a focus on engineering. These events include Engineering Week and STEM nights hosted at the museum. Since the beginning, I worked with others to curate an engineering activity for 500 people during engineering STEM night, and have done research on how visitors engaged with new activities to determine their success.

Currently, I'm working on developing new engineering activities for the museum based on learning goals for visitors. This task include research, protyping, designing, and surveying to determine how useful and engaging the activity is. Soon in April, I'll be working with an engineer, Krystal Persaud, and Design Residents to curate an exhibit about solar energy and sustainability in general.

NYSCI Career Ladder: Explainer

Employee• July 2017- Present

The New York Hall of Science is an interactive museum with the motto: Design Make Play. It is open-ended exploration to motivate STEM learning. I interact with these responsive hands-on STEM exhibits with museum guests, educate the general public about STEM topics, and facilitate educational programs for audiences in the Design Lab. Meanwhile, I gain communication skills, learn more about science, and teach these science topics to the museum public.

Science Ambassadors Program: Homework Help

Intern• March 2017- June 2017

The Science Ambassadors program is a new initiative at NYSCI that helps young Corona students develop kills such as problem solving, collaboration, and critical thinking. I worked in the Homework Help subprogram (March-June) where I guided students on their work, worked with students on a wide variety of subjects, actively engaged parents with the common core curriculum, and coordinated with coworkers to ensure success in our local corona students' education.

Hawthorne Program

Intern• July-August 2016

A summer program for students looking to get ahead of their studies or get involved in extracurricular activities. Here I tutored and assisted children of various ages individually and in small groups in order to help them master assignments and to reinforce learning concepts presented by teachers. I also instructed and monitored students in the use and care of equipment and materials, in order to prevent injuries and damage. Outside of the classroom, I assisted in daily arrival and dismissal by proactively greeting parents and students, directing them to the appropriate classrooms and answering any questions regarding the program.

Dream SHSI: Education Program

Teacher's Assistant• July-August 2014

A 22-month extracurricular program of rigorous coursework designed to assist eligible low-income, ambitious NYC public school students in preparing for the Specialized High Schools Admissions Test (SHSAT). I served as a Teacher's Assistant to help students with verbal and math skills, problem-solving, critical analysis, time management, and test-taking strategies specific to the SHSAT.
